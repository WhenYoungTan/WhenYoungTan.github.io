Goals

We all got to aim somewhere, don't we? This list below highlights the goals I have for my 2 years of national service.

Attain the NUS Merit Scholarship

Apply for a scholarship and make good use of my good grades. If offered, this scholarship would help to alleviate the financial stress on my family and give me a stronger sense of independence. It would also act as a testament to the hard work I've put in for my 'A' levels. Applied in 2021 but was not offered interview. Reapplied in 2022 and was offered on 12 May 2022. :D Legit the happiest day in a long while. Ty NUS ♥♥.

Have a basic understanding of programming

CS50x

And here I am, here in CS50. Frankly I'm really glad I've found this course. It gives a broad yet detailed view of programming, delving into how computers percieve and percieve our inputs and introducing us to a wide variety of languages. My thanks goes to harvard for providing such an informative course for free.

Build your own personal website

Wow. I'm doing that right now.
An impressive way to show off your portfolio is through your very own website and I would have never thought I would be making one so early. I also did not expect the commitment I was going to pour into this project.

Volunteer once a month

Now that my schedule is starting to free up, it'll be great if I could volunteer at least once a month. I've been holding back for quite a while as I could not find any friends to accompany me. :( Its still a work in progress, but with some dedication this should be easily achievable. I plan to start off with a soup kitchen since it seems to have a lot of open slots.

Cook more and learn new recipes

Start to cook on a regular basis and learn new dishes. I can no longer stand the taste of army food and I'm really tempted to start preparing my own food and bringing it into camp. Plus, now that I've gotten the scholarship, I could try cooking fancier and more expensive dishes like steaks or carbonara. (yummy :D)

Learn Arduino

Well since I'm pursuing Electrical Engineering as a career, it makes sense for me to learn and play around with Arduino. I have my eyes laid on a beginner course in skillshare, which I hope is informative and clear enough to get me started on an Arduino project.

Learn Electrical Engineering Fundamentals

This would give me a head-start in my university studies. EDX offers some courses in this and I plan to take them as soon as I'm finished with Arduino.

Participate in a team competition

Just for the experience. It has been a long while since I worked in a project with a team. Plus, it's also something to add to my portfolio.

Learn programming further in-depth

Why stop at CS50? CS50W and CS50AI sounds really interesting and if I do have the time, I would definetely pursue either one of them.

Pursue an internship

DSO

After I finish my service with the Ministry of Defence, I have have a gap period of 4 months before I matriculate in 2023. I plan to utilize the time with an internship at DSO to give me an insight into the defence industry and the expectations I should be having in my career.
